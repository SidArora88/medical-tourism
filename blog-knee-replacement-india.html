Knee Replacement Surgery in India

Complete guide to knee replacement surgery in India. Learn about total and partial knee replacement, robotic surgery, top hospitals, costs, and recovery for international patients.

Knee replacement surgery in India has transformed the lives of hundreds of thousands of patients worldwide, offering advanced orthopedic care at a fraction of international costs. With over 150,000 knee replacement procedures performed annually and success rates exceeding 95%, India has become the global leader in joint replacement surgery, combining cutting-edge technology with unparalleled expertise.

Understanding Knee Replacement Surgery

Knee replacement, also known as knee arthroplasty, involves replacing damaged or diseased knee joint surfaces with artificial implants. This procedure is recommended for patients with severe knee arthritis, injury, or other conditions that cause chronic pain and disability. Common conditions requiring knee replacement include:

  • Osteoarthritis: Wear-and-tear arthritis affecting joint cartilage
  • Rheumatoid Arthritis: Autoimmune condition causing joint inflammation
  • Post-traumatic Arthritis: Arthritis following knee injury
  • Avascular Necrosis: Bone death due to poor blood supply
  • Congenital Deformities: Birth defects affecting knee alignment

Types of Knee Replacement Procedures

Total Knee Replacement (TKR)

  • Complete replacement of knee joint surfaces
  • Best for advanced arthritis
  • Most common procedure performed
  • Excellent long-term outcomes
  • 15-20 year implant lifespan

Partial Knee Replacement (PKR)

  • Only damaged compartment replaced
  • Preserves healthy bone and ligaments
  • Smaller incision and faster recovery
  • More natural knee feel
  • Suitable for localized arthritis

Revision Knee Replacement

  • Replacement of failed previous implant
  • More complex surgical procedure
  • Specialized implants and techniques
  • Longer recovery period
  • Requires experienced surgeon

Robotic-Assisted Knee Replacement

  • Computer-guided precision surgery
  • Improved implant positioning
  • Better preservation of healthy tissue
  • Enhanced surgical accuracy
  • Potentially longer implant life

The Surgical Procedure

Traditional Knee Replacement

  1. Anesthesia: Spinal or general anesthesia administration
  2. Incision: 6-8 inch incision over the knee
  3. Bone Preparation: Damaged cartilage and bone removal
  4. Implant Placement: Artificial components secured to bone
  5. Balancing: Ligament tension and alignment optimization
  6. Closure: Layer-by-layer wound closure

Minimally Invasive Techniques

  • Smaller Incision: 3-4 inch incision
  • Muscle-Sparing Approach: Reduced tissue trauma
  • Faster Recovery: Earlier mobilization possible
  • Less Pain: Reduced post-operative discomfort
  • Shorter Hospital Stay: 2-3 days versus 4-5 days

Recovery and Rehabilitation

Day 1-2

Immediate Post-Op

Pain management, gentle leg exercises, and assisted standing. Physical therapy begins within 24 hours.

Week 1

Early Mobilization

Walking with walker or crutches, basic knee exercises, wound care, and discharge planning.

Week 2-6

Home Recovery

Progressive weight-bearing, outpatient physical therapy, and gradual activity increase.

Week 6-12

Strengthening Phase

Advanced exercises, return to light activities, and driving (if right knee replaced).

Month 3-6

Full Recovery

Return to normal activities, sports clearance, and long-term maintenance exercises.
